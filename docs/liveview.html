What air quaility are you measuring?

We measure air quality by the capturing level of particulate matter. This is produced by vehicle exhaust emissions, dust from vehicle brake linings and burning wood. The particulate matter is measured in 2/.5 and 10.0 Micron sizes, and in quantities of parts per million in the air, we breathe.

Why does air quaility matter?

More than five percent of ‘deaths’ in England are attributable to long-term exposure to particulate air pollution, according to the latest data published in the Public Health Outcomes Framework (PHOF). As a result, adverse health effects caused by air pollution are increasingly being at a national and international level.

What's the aim of the project?

Our aim is to empower the community with information on how vehicle traffic and other sources of pollutants can impact air quality over time. We hope that the information will enable people to make more informed choices about their lifestyle, and consider things that they can do to improve the environment.
